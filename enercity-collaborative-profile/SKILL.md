## Positioning

EnerCity Collaborative is a Portland, Oregon nonprofit building a more
inclusive clean energy economy from the ground up. We train and credential
people from BIPOC, underserved, and frontline communities — and the
minority contractors who employ them — for real careers in building
science, residential energy auditing, weatherization, and green
construction. As a certified Building Performance Institute (BPI) Test
Center, ECC delivers hands-on, culturally specific training that turns
expanding clean-energy funding into durable, healthy homes and durable,
family-sustaining careers. Our work is rooted in equity: we exist to
make sure the financial, environmental, and health benefits of the clean
energy transition reach the communities too often left out of it.

Mission (verbatim — quotable): _To ensure the financial, environmental,
and health benefits of a clean energy economy are fully realized in
BIPOC, underserved, and frontline communities._

Tagline: _Empowering voices in the clean energy economy._

## What we offer

ECC's offerings are training programs and workforce-development
services, not products. Most are grant-funded and free or low-cost to
participants — **do not quote prices or fees in posts** unless ECC
provides specific figures. Lead with access and outcomes.

### Workforce training & certification

- **BPI Building Analyst Technician (BA-T)** and **Building Analyst
  Professional (BA-P)** — residential energy auditing and building
  science, to current BPI standards. Delivered in cohorts.
- **BPI Healthy Home Evaluator** — in partnership with HeatSpring and
  Earth Advantage.
- **Sustainable Homes Professional (SHP)** — for remodel/retrofit and
  minority contractor organizations (e.g., LatinoBuilt, NAMC Oregon,
  PBDG), in collaboration with Earth Advantage.
- Certified **BPI Test Center** — ECC can test and credential
  professionals directly.

### Training focus areas

- **Building Science & Energy Auditing** — home performance,
  diagnostics, efficiency upgrades.
- **Green Construction Pathways** — students and emerging professionals
  in sustainable building practices.
- **Contractor & Workforce Development** — technical skills,
  certifications, and business-growth support for minority contractors
  and new trainees.

### Beyond training

- **Policy & advocacy** — advocacy training and public-education work to
  advance energy equity and healthy homes.
- **Community education** — increasing understanding of healthy,
  energy-efficient, durable homes in frontline communities.

## Content pillars — what ECC posts about

Topic selection must ladder up to one of these pillars. A post that
doesn't connect to a pillar is off-mission for ECC's LinkedIn presence,
even if it's trending in clean energy generally.

- **Cohort recruitment** — applications open, deadlines, "who this is
  for," how to apply for BA-T/BA-P/Healthy Home Evaluator/SHP cohorts.
- **Trainee & graduate spotlights** — milestones, certifications earned,
  career outcomes, cohort completions. ECC's strongest content.
- **Partner collaborations** — joint work with training/community
  partners (LatinoBuilt, NAMC Oregon, Constructing Hope, Oregon
  Tradeswomen, Portland Youth Builders, POIC, BluePrint Foundation,
  Green Workforce Academy/Ecotrust, Earth Advantage, PBDG).
- **Healthy homes & community health** — connecting the built
  environment to public health, durability, indoor air quality,
  affordability.
- **Clean-energy career pathways** — the case for green careers;
  weatherization, retrofitting, net-zero, energy auditing as accessible
  on-ramps.
- **Equity & energy justice** — why representation in the clean energy
  workforce matters; ECC's mission and impact.
- **Events & community presence** — workshops, info sessions, pitch
  events, tabling, panels.
- **Funder & policy moments** — celebrating support and advancing
  energy-equity policy, within the advocacy posture confirmed by ECC.

## What we avoid — off-mission / off-voice

**Principle**: skip anything that (a) doesn't ladder up to a content
pillar, (b) conflicts with ECC's equity mission or community-partner
posture, or (c) makes a claim ECC can't stand behind. ECC's reputation
with funders and frontline communities is the asset the LinkedIn
presence protects.

Avoid:

- **Partisan / electoral politics** beyond ECC's defined energy-equity
  and healthy-homes advocacy lane. **[TBD: confirm with ECC]** how far
  into policy advocacy public posts should go.
- **Unbacked claims, statistics, or outcomes** — no invented numbers,
  no certainty beyond the cited sources.
- **Greenwashing or vendor-style product pitches** — ECC trains people;
  it doesn't sell equipment or endorse specific commercial products
  without a clear, confirmed partnership.
- **Competitor or peer disparagement** — the sector is collaborative;
  ECC builds the field, it doesn't knock it.
- **Deficit/"rescue" framing** of BIPOC, immigrant, or low-income
  communities. Lead with agency and talent.
- **Off-geography content** with no Oregon / Portland connection and no
  tie to ECC's mission (see Geographic focus).

When a topic is a partial fit, judge it against the principle, not the
keyword. When in doubt, hold the post and flag it for human review.

## Geographic focus

ECC's programs and community are anchored in Oregon, with the Portland
metro area at the center (much of the local funding is Portland-specific;
some clean-energy programs are statewide Oregon).

- **Primary**: Portland metro area / Multnomah County and surrounding
  Oregon communities.
- **Secondary**: the rest of Oregon and the Portland–Willamette Valley
  corridor where programs and partners reach.
- **Framing test**: a post should connect to ECC's Oregon-based
  programs, partners, events, or community — not generic national
  clean-energy news with no local hook.
